Crossing from Hong Kong to Shenzhen at Lo Wu vs. Futian

Which border to take, when to go, what to expect at immigration, and the metro tricks that save you 40 minutes on the Shenzhen side.

Two crossings, different trade-offs

There are several land border crossings between Hong Kong and Shenzhen, but for visitors staying in central Shenzhen, two matter: Lo Wu and Futian. They're about four kilometers apart, served by different MTR stations in Hong Kong, and they connect to different parts of Shenzhen's metro on the other side. The right one depends on where you're headed and what time you're crossing.

Lo Wu

Lo Wu is the older crossing and historically the busiest. It sits at the end of Hong Kong's East Rail Line — a straight shot from Kowloon or Hong Kong station with no transfers. On the Shenzhen side, you walk out into Lo Wu Station on Shenzhen Metro Line 1, which runs straight into the city center (Luohu, Huaqiangbei, Futian CBD).

The downside is volume. Lo Wu processes a huge share of cross-border commuter traffic, and during peak hours — weekday mornings, Friday evenings, and any Chinese public holiday — queues at immigration can stretch 45 minutes or more. If you're crossing between 7am and 9am or after 5pm on a weekday, expect a wait.

Hours: 6:30am – midnight.

Futian

Futian is the newer crossing and, for most tourists, the better choice. It's underground — the only subterranean border crossing in the world — which means no outdoor queuing in humidity. Passenger volume is lower than Lo Wu, and immigration is faster outside of peak windows.

Getting there from Hong Kong requires one transfer: take the East Rail to Sheung Shui, then switch to the Light Rail or a connecting service to Lok Ma Chau, which connects to Futian. It adds about 15 minutes of travel time versus Lo Wu, but you'll often save more than that at immigration.

On the Shenzhen side, Futian Port Station connects directly to Line 4, which runs north into Futian CBD — the commercial and tech district where many of our visits are based. If you're heading to Huaqiangbei (the electronics market), it's two stops on Line 4 to transfer to Line 1.

Hours: 6:30am – 10:30pm. Note the earlier closing time versus Lo Wu.

What to expect at immigration

Both crossings follow the same process: Hong Kong departure card, passport check on the HK side, walk across a short bridge or corridor, then mainland China arrival card and passport control. Have your visa (or visa-free documentation) ready. The mainland officers are efficient but not chatty — answer questions simply and directly.

First-timers are sometimes surprised that you clear two full immigration checkpoints, not one. Budget 20–40 minutes for the crossing under normal conditions, 60+ during holidays.

The fastest route into central Shenzhen

If you're heading to Futian CBD or Huaqiangbei: cross at Futian, take Line 4 north two stops to Huaqiang North, and you're there. Total transit time from Tsim Sha Tsui in Kowloon: about 70 minutes on a good day.

If you're heading to Luohu or the eastern part of the city: Lo Wu is marginally better, and Line 1 will get you there directly. Just cross before 8am or after 10am to avoid the worst of the queues.

We use Futian for our group crossings. It's predictable, fast, and drops you close to where the action is.
